# 1-Minute Entry Check

## What This Is

The 1-Minute Entry Check is a quick entrance check before starting an AI work loop.

Use it before:

- giving a new task to an AI assistant
- restarting paused work
- handing a repo to another AI assistant
- deciding whether a pain/opportunity is ready for entry

It helps the human Decision Owner decide how to enter the next AI work loop.

## What This Is Not

This check is not:

- a build command
- market timing proof
- implementation approval
- external posting permission
- paid-offer permission
- automation
- an API, scanner, scraper, runtime, or chart renderer
- a replacement for the Decision Owner

It does not decide what to build.
It does not guarantee market timing.
It does not authorize implementation.
It does not replace the Decision Owner.

## The 5 Questions

1. Is this the right time to enter?
2. Is the gate `GO`, `SOFT GO`, `HOLD`, or `BLOCK`?
3. If something feels off, is it opportunity loss, wasted work, or an accident signal?
4. If this is interrupted work, can it be safely resumed?
5. What is the smallest next action?

## How Entry Uses Pain And Re-entry

Entry is the entrance check.

Pain can support Entry when the question is about friction, waste, opportunity loss, cost, avoidance, or accident signals.

Re-entry can support Entry when the work is interrupted, old, unclear, or being handed to another AI assistant.

Entry should not pretend to be Pain or Re-entry:

- Pain helps identify what hurts and why it may matter.
- Re-entry helps determine whether old or interrupted work can resume safely.
- Entry decides how strongly to enter the next work loop.

## Example Output

```text
Entry Gate: SOFT GO
Reason: The pain is understandable and the work can start, but the evidence is still thin.
Friction Signal: Wasted work risk if the AI assistant starts without a clear next action.
Re-entry State: Resumable with a short checkpoint.
Smallest Next Action: Write one Entry Decision Card before asking the AI assistant to change files.
Do Not Do: Do not start implementation, posting, pricing, automation, or repo expansion from this check.
Recheck Condition: Recheck after one bounded AI work loop produces a concrete artifact or exposes a blocking unknown.
Decision Owner: Human
```

## Responsibility Boundary

The 1-Minute Entry Check helps choose the next entrance posture.
It does not create a pain signal, guarantee market timing, approve implementation, or decide what to build.

The human remains the Decision Owner.

## Completion Line

The 1-Minute Entry Check gives first-time users a low-friction way to decide whether to enter, enter softly, hold, or block before handing work to an AI assistant.
